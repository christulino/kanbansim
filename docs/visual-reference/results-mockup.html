Lead Time & Throughput vs. WIP Limit

In Progress WIP swept from 1 to 15. Bands = 5th–95th percentile across 10,000 runs.
A clean U-curve. Below WIP ≈ 4, the team is starved — workers idle when items block, and throughput suffers. Above WIP ≈ 8, multitasking tax dominates and lead time blows up. The cliff is real, the floor is real, and the sweet spot is broader than most teams assume — that's the manager's permission to experiment.

The distribution is right-skewed (as real cycle times always are). When you tell a stakeholder "lead time is 12 days," you're describing the median — but 1 in 20 items takes 27 days or more. That tail is what teams need to plan around, not the mean.

At WIP=12 the team works half as much on actual items — the rest evaporates into context-switching and blocked-waiting. This is the multitasking tax made visible. Idle time is low at high WIP not because work is getting done, but because workers are always juggling something.
